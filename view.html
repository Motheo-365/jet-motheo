Airbus A320

The Airbus A320 is a narrow-body, twin-engine jet developed by Airbus and introduced in 1988. It is one of the most successful and widely used commercial aircraft in the world. Designed primarily for short- to medium-haul routes, the A320 typically seats between 150 and 180 passengers depending on configuration.

The aircraft was the first commercial jet to introduce a full digital fly-by-wire control system, improving safety and efficiency. It is powered by either CFM International or Pratt & Whitney engines and is known for fuel efficiency, reliability, and low operating costs.

  • Max Range: Approximately 6,300 km
  • Max Speed: 492 knots (900 km/h)
  • Typical Seating: 150–180 passengers
  • Cabin Classes: Economy, Business
  • Cargo Capacity: 36.5 cubic meters
  • Engines: CFM56 or PW1000G (neo version)
  • Primary Use: Short to medium-haul routes

Airbus A319

The Airbus A319 is a shorter variant of the A320, designed for routes with lower passenger demand. It entered service in 1996 and typically seats between 120 and 150 passengers.

Because of its smaller size, the A319 can operate from shorter runways and airports with operational constraints, making it popular among airlines operating regional and thinner routes.

  • Max Range: Approximately 6,900 km
  • Max Speed: 492 knots (900 km/h)
  • Typical Seating: 120–150 passengers
  • Cabin Classes: Economy, Business
  • Cargo Capacity: 32.4 cubic meters
  • Engines: CFM56 or V2500
  • Primary Use: Regional and medium-haul routes

Boeing 737-800

The Boeing 737-800 is part of the Boeing 737 Next Generation (NG) family and is one of the best-selling aircraft in aviation history. It entered service in 1998 and competes directly with the Airbus A320.

The aircraft is widely used by both full-service and low-cost carriers. It is known for its operational flexibility, fuel efficiency, and reliability on short- to medium-haul routes.

  • Max Range: Approximately 5,400 km
  • Max Speed: 488 knots (904 km/h)
  • Typical Seating: 162–189 passengers
  • Cabin Classes: Economy, Business
  • Cargo Capacity: 34.2 cubic meters
  • Engines: CFM56-7B
  • Primary Use: Short to medium-haul routes

Embraer E190

The Embraer E190 is a narrow-body regional jet manufactured by the Brazilian company Embraer. It entered service in 2004 and is part of the E-Jet family.

The E190 typically seats between 88 and 114 passengers and is designed for short- to medium-haul routes. It offers airlines the flexibility to serve smaller markets while maintaining jet comfort.

  • Max Range: Approximately 4,500 km
  • Max Speed: 511 knots (946 km/h)
  • Typical Seating: 88–114 passengers
  • Cabin Classes: Economy
  • Cargo Capacity: 14 cubic meters
  • Engines: GE CF34-10E
  • Primary Use: Regional and short-haul routes

Airbus A330

The Airbus A330 is a wide-body, twin-engine aircraft designed for medium- to long-haul routes. It entered service in 1994 and is used extensively for international travel.

The A330 can carry between 250 and 300 passengers depending on configuration and is known for its comfort and long-range capabilities. It is commonly used for transcontinental and intercontinental flights.

  • Max Range: Approximately 13,450 km (variant dependent)
  • Max Speed: 490 knots (908 km/h)
  • Typical Seating: 250–300 passengers
  • Cabin Classes: Economy, Business, First
  • Cargo Capacity: 139 cubic meters
  • Engines: Rolls-Royce Trent 700, GE CF6, or PW4000
  • Primary Use: Long-haul international routes

Airbus A350

The Airbus A350 is a modern wide-body aircraft designed for long-haul and ultra-long-haul routes. It entered service in 2015 and features advanced composite materials, which reduce weight and improve fuel efficiency.

The A350 offers improved passenger comfort, including higher cabin humidity, larger windows, and reduced noise levels. It typically seats between 300 and 410 passengers depending on variant.

  • Max Range: Up to 15,000 km
  • Max Speed: 490 knots (908 km/h)
  • Typical Seating: 300–410 passengers
  • Cabin Classes: Economy, Business, First
  • Cargo Capacity: 156 cubic meters
  • Engines: Rolls-Royce Trent XWB
  • Primary Use: Long-haul and ultra-long-haul routes

Boeing 787 Dreamliner

The Boeing 787 Dreamliner is a long-haul, wide-body aircraft introduced in 2011. It was designed with fuel efficiency and passenger comfort as primary goals.

The aircraft uses lightweight composite materials and features large electronically dimmable windows, improved cabin pressurization, and enhanced air filtration systems. It is used on medium- to long-haul international routes.

  • Max Range: Approximately 13,600–14,800 km (variant dependent)
  • Max Speed: 490 knots (909 km/h)
  • Typical Seating: 242–335 passengers
  • Cabin Classes: Economy, Business, First
  • Cargo Capacity: 172 cubic meters
  • Engines: Rolls-Royce Trent 1000 or GE GEnx
  • Primary Use: Long-haul international routes
